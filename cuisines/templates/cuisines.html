Indian Cuisines

North Indian Cuisine

Known for its rich, creamy gravies, tandoori dishes, and bread like naan and roti. North Indian cuisine often uses dairy products like ghee and paneer extensively. Dishes like butter chicken, tandoori chicken, and various types of kebabs are popular in this region.

South Indian Cuisine

Characterized by its generous use of rice, lentils, coconut, and spices like curry leaves, mustard seeds, and dried red chilies. South Indian cuisine includes dishes like dosa, idli, sambhar, and rasam. It tends to be lighter and often vegetarian compared to North Indian cuisine.

East Indian Cuisine

Influenced by its proximity to neighboring countries like Bangladesh and Myanmar, East Indian cuisine is known for its use of fish, rice, and mustard oil. Dishes like fish curry, pitha (rice cakes), and various chutneys are common in this region.

West Indian Cuisine

Known for its vibrant flavors and eclectic mix of ingredients. West Indian cuisine includes dishes from Gujarat, Maharashtra, and Goa. Gujarati cuisine is predominantly vegetarian, with dishes like dhokla and thepla being popular. Maharashtrian cuisine features spicy curries, street foods like vada pav, and seafood specialties in coastal areas. Goan cuisine is influenced by Portuguese colonization and is famous for its use of coconut, seafood, and spices in dishes like vindaloo and xacuti.

Punjabi Cuisine

Hailing from the Punjab region in North India, Punjabi cuisine is characterized by its robust flavors and generous use of dairy products like ghee, butter, and cream. Dishes like sarson da saag (mustard greens curry), makki di roti (cornmeal flatbread), and lassi (yogurt drink) are staples of Punjabi cuisine.

Bengali Cuisine

Known for its emphasis on seafood, rice, and sweets. Bengali cuisine features dishes like macher jhol (fish curry), kosha mangsho (spicy mutton curry), and sweets like rasgulla and sandesh.

Hyderabadi Cuisine

Influenced by Mughlai and Persian cuisines, Hyderabadi cuisine is famous for its biryanis, particularly the Hyderabadi biryani made with basmati rice, meat (usually chicken or mutton), and a blend of spices.
