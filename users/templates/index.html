What is Phagyul?

Since at least 13th century, several influential indigenous Tibetan lamas & authors also started to refer to India as the Phagyul, short for Phags yul, meaning the land of aryas i.e. land of noble, holy, enlightened & superior people who are the source of spiritual enlightenment.

Exploring the rich tapestry of Indian Culture

India, a land of staggering diversity, offers a kaleidoscope of experiences that range from the snow-capped Himalayas in the north to the sun-drenched beaches of the south. It’s a country where every turn is a new adventure, every festival a celebration of life, and every meal a tapestry of flavors.

Cuisines

Indian cuisine is not just food; it’s a voyage. Each dish tells a story of regional heritage, infused with spices that dance on the palate. From the robust flavors of Punjab to the coastal aromas of Kerala, Indian food is a boundless journey of taste.

Dance Forms

The rhythm of India is expressed in its dance. Each form, from the intricate footwork of Kathak to the expressive gestures of Bharatanatyam, is a window into India’s soul. Dance in India is more than art—it’s a spiritual dialogue.

Monuments

The stones of India’s monuments whisper tales of yore. The majestic Taj Mahal, the enduring forts of Rajasthan, and the ancient temples of Tamil Nadu stand as testaments to a history that shaped the world.
