The Nagara style of temple architecture is primarily characterized by its tall and curvilinear spires (Shikharas), often adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures. These temples typically have a square base and gradually taper upwards. Examples of Nagara style temples include the Lingaraja Temple in Bhubaneswar and the Kandariya Mahadeva Temple in Khajuraho.

The Dravida style of temple architecture is known for its pyramidal or stepped temple towers (Vimanas) that rise gradually in a series of terraces. These temples often have intricate sculptures and carvings depicting mythological stories. Prominent examples of Dravida style temples include the Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur and the Meenakshi Temple in Madurai.
